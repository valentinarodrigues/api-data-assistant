# Our first post-PI refinement session just felt... different. Here's why.

Last week was PI Planning week.

Boards full. Energy high. Everyone aligned. ✅

You know the feeling.

Then this Wednesday arrives.

First sprint refinement session post-PI.  
The moment where that energy either holds…  
or quietly dies in a room full of half-written tickets.

We've all been there 👇

> *"Wait, what does this story actually mean?"*  
> *"Who assigned this label?"*  
> *"Does this have acceptance criteria?"*  
> *45 minutes later — still on ticket 3.*

**Not this time.**

---

We built an AI agent connected to Jira via MCP that processed every single ticket in the PI before anyone sat down.

Not some of them. **Every. Single. One.**

For each ticket the agent:

- ✅ Drafted story descriptions and acceptance criteria
- ✅ Suggested SCORPS and HPE tags based on ticket evidence
- ✅ Enforced our Definition of Ready automatically
- ✅ Generated an epic-by-epic summary ready for the room

**The agent prepares. The team decides.**  
That's the bit that matters.

---

## 🔷 Why tagging matters more than people think

We run two internal review sessions:

**SCORPS meetings** — we sit together and align each ticket to its SCORPS pillar  
**HPE reviews** — same process, different lens

These sessions drive alignment, reporting and decision-making across the whole team.

When tags are missing or inconsistent, those conversations get noisy fast.

The agent suggests. The team reviews together. **We own the outcome.**

---

## The result this Wednesday?

Not a single ticket rewritten in the room.

The team talked about **risk**, **dependencies** and **scope.**  
The stuff we're actually here for.

---

Will this work the same way for every team? Probably not.  
Different setups. Different ways of working.

But this is what worked for us — and it felt worth sharing.

> *The goal was never faster meetings.*  
> *It was better ones.*  
> *This Wednesday felt like we finally got there.* 🎯
