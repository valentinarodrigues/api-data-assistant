# Our first post-PI refinement session just felt... different. Here's why.

Last week was PI Planning week.
Boards full. Energy high. Everyone aligned. ✅
You know the feeling.

Then this Wednesday arrives.

First sprint refinement session post-PI. The moment where that energy either holds… or quietly dies in a room full of half-written tickets.

We've all been there.

"Wait, what does this story actually mean?"
"Who assigned this label?"
"Does this have acceptance criteria?"
45 minutes later — still on ticket 3.

Not this time.

## Here's what changed.

We built an AI agent — using Jira MCP and Rovo — that processed every single ticket in the PI before anyone sat down.

Not some of them. All of them.

For each ticket it:
- ✅ Drafted story descriptions and acceptance criteria
- ✅ Suggested SCORPS and HPE tags based on ticket evidence
- ✅ Enforced our Definition of Ready automatically
- ✅ Generated an epic-by-epic summary so the team could walk in with full context

And the human stayed in the loop throughout. The agent doesn't decide — it prepares. The team reviews, challenges and confirms. That's the bit that matters.

## Here's why the tagging piece is bigger than it sounds.

We have two internal review sessions:
- 🔷 **SCORPS meetings** — where we sit as a team and work through which tickets align to each SCORPS pillar
- 🔶 **HPE reviews** — same process, different lens

These aren't just admin exercises. They drive alignment, reporting and decision-making across the team. When tags are inconsistent or missing, those meetings slow down fast.

The agent suggests the tags. The team reviews them together. We own the outcome.

That's the human in the loop — not rubber stamping, actually thinking.

## The result this Wednesday?

We didn't rewrite a single ticket.

We walked in, reviewed epic by epic, talked about risk, dependencies and scope — and left with everything tagged, refined and ready.

Will this work the same way for every team? Probably not.
Different teams. Different tools. Different ways of working.

But for us, right now, this is what worked.

The goal was never faster meetings.
It was better ones.

This Wednesday felt like we finally got there. 🎯
